8.1 Simple and Compound Interest

What is Interest?

Interest – the cost of borrowing money OR the reward for saving/investing money.

Cost of Borrowing

Extra money you pay when you borrow from banks or lenders

Reward for Saving

Extra money you earn when you save or invest your money

Two Main Types:

Simple Interest

Compound Interest

Simple Interest

Simple Interest grows at a constant rate over time. It is based only on the original amount (principal).

Formula:

I = P × r × t

P

Principal (initial money)

r

Rate (interest rate per year)

t

Time (years)

I

Interest earned

Used For:

Short-term loans

Some salary loans

Car loans (sometimes)

Simple Interest Example

Scenario:

You borrowed ₱10,000 at 5% for 2 years.

I = 10,000 × 0.05 × 2

= ₱1,000 interest

Total to pay = ₱11,000

Key Characteristic: Interest grows linearly (straight line)

Compound Interest

Compound Interest means interest grows on top of previous interest.
"Interest on interest."

Formula:

A = P(1 + r/n)nt

A

Final amount

P

Principal

r

Rate

n

Compounding per year

t

Time

Used For:

Savings accounts

Investments

Credit card debt

Loans with monthly payments

Compound Interest Example

Scenario:

₱10,000 at 5% compounded yearly for 2 years:

A = 10,000 × (1.05)2

A = 10,000 × 1.1025

= ₱11,025

Notice: ₱1,025 > ₱1,000 (simple interest)

Key Characteristic: Interest grows exponentially

Key Insight:

Compound interest makes your money grow faster because you earn interest on your interest!
