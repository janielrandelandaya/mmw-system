Home Ownership

Buying a house or property using a combination of savings and financing

How It Works:

Down Payment

Initial cash payment (usually 20%)

Mortgage Loan

Bank loan for the remaining amount

Benefits of Home Ownership:

Long-term Asset

Property value typically increases over time

Builds Equity

You own more of your home over time

Family Stability

Permanent home for your family

Mortgage Loan

Mortgage = long-term loan used to buy a house

Common Loan Terms:

10 years

Short term

15 years

Medium term

20 years

Long term

30 years

Very long term

Key Features:

Monthly Amortization

Fixed monthly payments over the loan term

Interest Types

Fixed or variable interest rates available

Important:

Most of the early payments go to interest, not principal

It takes several years before your payments significantly reduce the actual loan amount

Mortgage Example

Sample Home Purchase:

Purchase Details:

House price: ₱2,000,000
Down payment (20%): ₱400,000
Loan amount: ₱1,600,000
Interest rate: 7% per year
Loan period: 20 years

Payment Calculation:

Monthly payment: ≈ ₱12,403/month
Total payments: ₱2,976,720
Total interest: ₱1,376,720

Key Insight:

Total paid over 20 years ≈ ₱2,976,720

→ Big part is interest (₱1,376,720 interest vs ₱1,600,000 principal)

Breakdown:

You pay 86% more than the original loan amount due to interest!
